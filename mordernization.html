3. The true about "GMO"
The first GMO was released in 1994, and it was commercialized in 1996. So, it has been about
30 years now. According to a report published in "the national academies of sciences
engineering and medicine", no risk was found among currently commercial GM plants and
it was reported to be as safe as general crops. Opponents will probably say that there are
papers stating that GMOs are harmful to the human body. There are. Representatively, according
to a paper published in 2012 at the University of Caen in France, mice that ate GM corn died
2-3 times faster than mice that did not eat them, and tumors or cancers were also found.
However, regarding this paper, the experimental method was wrong, and the conclusion was
not clear, resulting in withdrawal.Another piece of evidence for the opponents is the Brazil
nut case, developed by an American seed company, which probably actually had side effects.
It introduced a gene called "2s albumin" to develop genetically modified soybeans, which could
cause allergic reactions in people who ate the soybeans. So, it was reviewed to see if it caused
an allergy to soybeans containing "2s albumin", but the original 2s albumin itself was an
allergy-causing substance, so it was not caused by genetic modification, but an allergy to the
original 2s albumin. It was determined that it was caused. So, in simple terms, it is the result
of adding an allergic reaction substance, not the result of the genetic modification process.
Despite widespread belief among scientists that genetically engineered (GE) foods are safe
